Immigration in Panama

Panama's Pensionado visa is one of the world's most generous retirement visa programmes — it grants immediate permanent residency and includes significant discounts. Social Security income qualifies. Requirements subject to change.

Other visa options: The Friendly Nations Visa is available to citizens of 50+ countries including the US and grants permanent residency through economic ties or professional employment in Panama.

Understanding your US tax obligations

FEIE eligibilityYes — earned income only

FBAR required?Yes — if foreign accounts exceed $10,000 aggregate
Panama territorial taxPanama only taxes Panama-source income. US pensions, SS, and foreign investment income are NOT taxed by Panama.
Panama's territorial tax system is highly favourable for American retirees: Panama does not tax foreign-source income. Your US Social Security, pension, IRA withdrawals, and US investment income are not subject to Panamanian income tax. The US dollar also eliminates currency risk. This combination makes Panama one of the most straightforward tax situations for American retirees among popular destinations.

Safety & healthcare

Safety and healthcare for American expats

Safety
GPI Rank #67
Panama ranks mid-table on the GPI. Panama City has areas that vary significantly in safety. Most expats live in safe areas like Punta Pacifica, Costa del Este, or El Cangrejo. Research your specific neighbourhood carefully. US State Dept advisory: Level 2 — Exercise Increased Caution.
US State Dept advisory →
Healthcare
🏥 Private recommended
Panama City has excellent private hospitals including Johns Hopkins-affiliated Pacífica Salud. Medical costs are significantly lower than the US. The Pensionado visa includes healthcare discounts.
Medicare abroad: Original Medicare does not cover routine care in Panama. Plan your healthcare transition carefully before you leave the US.
